Recipe 4

Ingredients

  • 1 cup milk powder
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 2-3 tablespoons milk (as needed to form a dough)
  • Oil or ghee for deep frying
  • 1.5 cups sugar
  • 1.5 cups water
  • 1/2 teaspoon rose water
  • A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
  • Chopped pistachios or almonds for garnishing

Instructions

  • Step 1:
    In a mixing bowl, combine the milk powder, all-purpose flour, and baking powder. Mix well.
  • Step 2:
    Add the ghee to the dry mixture and rub it in with your fingers until it resembles coarse crumbs.
  • Step 3:
    Gradually add the milk, a tablespoon at a time, and knead the mixture into a soft and smooth dough. Be careful not to overwork the dough.
  • Step 4:
    Divide the dough into small equal-sized portions and roll them into smooth balls. Make sure there are no cracks on the surface.
  • Step 5:
    Heat the oil or ghee in a deep pan or kadai over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, reduce the heat to low and carefully add the gulab jamun balls to the oil. Fry them in batches, ensuring they have enough space to expand.
  • Step 6:
    Fry the gulab jamun balls until they turn golden brown, stirring occasionally for even cooking. It may take about 7-8 minutes. Remove them using a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on a paper towel.
  • Step 7:
    In a separate saucepan, combine the sugar and water. Bring it to a boil and let it simmer for about 5 minutes until the sugar completely dissolves and the syrup slightly thickens. Add the rose water and saffron strands (if using), and stir well.
  • Step 8:
    Add the fried gulab jamun balls to the warm syrup. Let them soak for at least 30 minutes to 1 hour, allowing the balls to absorb the syrup and become soft.
  • Step 9:
    Garnish the gulab jamun with chopped pistachios or almonds.
