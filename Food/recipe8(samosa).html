Recipe 8

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Water (as needed)
  • 2 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1/2 cup green peas
  • 1/2 cup carrots, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions

  • Step 1:
    In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour and salt. Add the vegetable oil and mix well. Gradually add water and knead until you have a smooth and firm dough. Cover the dough and let it rest for 20-30 minutes.
  • Step 2:
    In a pan, heat some oil over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  • Step 3:
    Add the chopped onions, minced garlic, and grated ginger. Sauté until the onions turn translucent.
  • Step 4:
    Add the carrots, green peas, coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for a few minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  • Step 5:
    Add the mashed potatoes and garam masala. Mix everything together until well combined. Remove from heat and let the filling cool.
  • Step 6:
    Divide the dough into small lemon-sized balls. Roll out each ball into a thin circle.
  • Step 7:
    Cut the rolled dough circle in half. Take one half and fold it into a cone shape, overlapping the edges. Seal the edges by applying a little water.
  • Step 8:
    Fill the cone with a spoonful of the vegetable filling. Make sure not to overfill it.
  • Step 9:
    Apply water on the edges of the cone and press to seal it completely. Repeat the process for the remaining dough and filling.
  • Step 10:
    Heat oil in a deep pan or skillet for frying. Once the oil is hot, carefully place a few samosas into the oil and fry them on medium heat until they turn golden brown and crispy. Flip them occasionally for even cooking.
  • Step 11:
    Once cooked, remove the samosas from the oil and drain them on a paper towel to remove excess oil.
  • Step 12:
    Serve the vegetable samosas hot with chutney or sauce of your choice. Enjoy these delicious and crispy snacks!
