Recipe 2

Ingredients

  • 250 grams paneer (Indian cottage cheese), cut into cubes
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 tablespoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • Salt to taste
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  • Step 1:
    Heat the butter and oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds and let them splutter
  • Step 2:
    Add the chopped onions to the pan and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  • Step 3:
    Add the ginger-garlic paste to the pan and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  • Step 4:
    Pour in the tomato puree and cook for a few minutes until the mixture thickens and the oil separates from the sides.
  • Step 5:
    Add the red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala powder, and salt to the pan. Stir well to combine the spices with the tomato mixture.
  • Step 6:
    Reduce the heat to low and add the heavy cream to the pan. Mix well and let it simmer for a couple of minutes.
  • Step 7:
    Gradually add the milk to the pan, stirring continuously. This helps to achieve the desired consistency of the gravy.
  • Step 8:
    Add the kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves) to the pan and mix it in. Crush the leaves between your palms before adding to enhance the flavor
  • Step 9:
    Gently add the paneer cubes to the gravy and stir to coat them with the sauce. Simmer for another 5 minutes, allowing the paneer to absorb the flavors.
  • Step 10:
    Taste the butter paneer and adjust the seasoning if needed.
  • Step 11:
    Once the paneer is heated through, remove the pan from heat.
  • Step 12:
    Garnish the butter paneer with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
