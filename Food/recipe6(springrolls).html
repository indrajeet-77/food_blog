Recipe 6

Ingredients

  • 12 spring roll wrappers
  • 1 cup shredded cabbage
  • 1 cup shredded carrots
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 1/2 cup thinly sliced bell peppers
  • 1/2 cup sliced mushrooms.
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • Oil for frying
  • Dipping sauce of your choice (such as sweet chili sauce or soy sauce)

Instructions

  • Step 1:
    In a large bowl, combine the cabbage, carrots, bean sprouts, bell peppers, mushrooms, green onions, and minced garlic.
  • Step 2:
    Add the soy sauce, oyster sauce (if using), sesame oil, and black pepper to the bowl. Toss the ingredients well, ensuring the vegetables are evenly coated with the seasoning.
  • Step 3:
    Fill a shallow dish or pan with warm water. Dip one spring roll wrapper into the water for a few seconds until it becomes pliable. Place it on a clean work surface.
  • Step 4:
    Spoon about 2 tablespoons of the vegetable filling onto the lower third of the spring roll wrapper, leaving a small border on the sides.
  • Step 5:
    Fold the sides of the wrapper over the filling, then tightly roll it up from the bottom to enclose the filling completely. Seal the edge of the wrapper by lightly brushing it with water
  • Step 6:
    Repeat the process with the remaining spring roll wrappers and filling.
  • Step 7:
    Heat oil in a deep pan or wok over medium heat. Carefully place a few spring rolls into the hot oil, seam side down.
  • Step 8:
    Fry the spring rolls for about 2-3 minutes on each side until they turn golden brown and crispy. Remove them with a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on a paper towel.
  • Step 9:
    Repeat the frying process with the remaining spring rolls
  • Step 10:
    Serve the spring rolls hot with your choice of dipping sauce.
  • Step 11:
    Enjoy your homemade spring rolls as an appetizer or snack! They're best when served fresh and crispy.
